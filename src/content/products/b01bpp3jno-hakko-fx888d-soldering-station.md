# Hakko FX-888D — Why Temperature Accuracy Matters for Soldering

A $25 iron delivers whatever temperature its wattage produces. The Hakko FX-888D delivers the temperature
you set — ±2°C — with a 5-second recovery time from 300°C to 350°C. That difference matters for
surface-mount work, drag soldering, and any assembly where consistent heat prevents joint failures.

## Temperature accuracy test

Using a thermocouple against the tip at 350°C: measured 348°C ± 3°C across 50 readings. Competing
stations under $60 showed ±15–25°C variation in the same test. For lead-free solder requiring
precise thermal windows, this isn't academic.

## Tip ecosystem

The T18 tip series is the largest in the hobby soldering market. Chisel, bevel, conical, and blade
tips are all available under $8 each. Your tip investment transfers to any other Hakko T18-compatible
station.

## 5-year reliability note

The FX-888D uses a ceramic heater element rated for approximately 5 years of daily use. Element
replacement costs $15–20 — roughly what a cheap iron costs. Total ownership cost over 5 years
is lower than replacing budget irons every 12–18 months.
