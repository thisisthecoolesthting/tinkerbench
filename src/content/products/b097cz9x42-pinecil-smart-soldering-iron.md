# Pinecil V2 — The USB-C Soldering Iron That Changed Portable Work

The Pinecil V2 runs on any 20V USB-C Power Delivery source. At a makerspace, your laptop charger
powers it. In the field, a 65W USB-C power bank provides 90+ minutes of soldering at 350°C.
No proprietary adapter, no wall wart, no DC barrel connector.

## Heat-up time

From cold to 350°C: 23 seconds on a 65W USB-C PD source. The original TS100 took 38 seconds.
A station iron like the FX-888D takes 40–60 seconds. The Pinecil wins on cold-start for field use.

## Tip compatibility

The Pinecil uses TS tip series, the most available hobby iron tip standard after Hakko T18.
Replacement tips run $3–6 each. The iron ships with a BC2 chisel tip adequate for general through-hole work.

## Where it loses to a station

Thermal mass is lower than a station iron's ceramic heater. When soldering large ground planes or
heavy gauge wire (14AWG+), the Pinecil struggles to maintain temperature under continuous draw.
For bench work involving heavy power connections, a station iron is still better. For everything
under 20AWG, the Pinecil competes fully.
