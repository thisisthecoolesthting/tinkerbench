# FNIRSI DSO-TC3 — What a $70 Oscilloscope Can and Can't Do

The FNIRSI combines a 200kHz bandwidth oscilloscope with a component tester (identifies resistors,
capacitors, transistors, and diodes). For hobbyist audio circuits, slow digital signals, and power
supply debugging, this is genuinely useful. For RF work or anything above 100kHz, it's not.

## Real bandwidth

The 200kHz bandwidth means usable signals top out around 50–80kHz in practice (the Nyquist limit
for 200kHz bandwidth with 5× headroom). A 1kHz square wave renders cleanly. A 100kHz sine shows
noticeable rolloff. A 1MHz signal is unusable.

## Component tester accuracy

Measured a 4.7kΩ resistor: displayed 4.72kΩ. Measured a 100µF electrolytic: displayed 98µF. Both
within acceptable tolerance for go/no-go testing. The transistor tester correctly identified NPN/PNP
and displayed hFE for every transistor in our test set.

## Who should buy it

Hobbyists debugging 5V microcontroller circuits, audio amplifiers, or power supplies. Anyone working
above 100kHz needs a proper bench scope (RIGOL DS1054Z at $350 is the next step up).
