## Best Benchtop Power Supply Under $150: Six Reliable Models Compared

A benchtop power supply is a fundamental tool for electronics hobbyists, students, and professionals alike. It provides a stable and adjustable source of DC power, essential for testing circuits, powering prototypes, and learning electronics safely. However, high-end models can be quite expensive, especially for beginners or those on a budget.

In this article, we'll explore six of the best benchtop power supplies you can find under $150. These models strike a good balance of features, performance, and value, making them suitable for various low- to mid-power applications without breaking the bank.

---

## What to Look for in a Budget Benchtop Power Supply

Before diving into the models, here are some factors to consider when shopping for an affordable benchtop power supply:

- **Output Voltage and Current:** Common ranges like 0-30V and 0-5A cover most low-power projects.
- **Display Type:** Digital displays provide better precision and readability than analog.
- **Regulation and Stability:** Check if the supply offers constant voltage (CV) and constant current (CC) modes.
- **Protection Features:** Overload, short circuit, and thermal protection improve safety.
- **Size and Build Quality:** For repeated use on a workbench, sturdiness counts.
- **Additional Features:** USB ports, multiple outputs, or fine adjustment knobs can be handy but may come at extra cost.

---

## 1. Basic Single-Output Digital Power Supply (30V 5A)

A no-frills, budget-friendly model offering a single output channel with 30V and 5A max ratings. This is usually enough for most beginner projects and general-purpose electronics work.

- **Pros:** 
  - Clear digital display for voltage and current.
  - Compact footprint ideal for limited bench space.
  - Basic CV/CC operation.
- **Cons:** 
  - Limited to one output channel.
  - Build quality varies by manufacturer; some units may feel lightweight.

This type of unit is great if you want straightforward, reliable power without bells and whistles.

---

## 2. Dual Output Adjustable Supply (2 Channels, Up to 30V 2A Each)

Having two independent outputs lets you power multiple circuits simultaneously at different voltages. Models in this category usually offer slightly lower current per channel but more flexibility.

- **Pros:**
  - Independent voltage and current control on each channel.
  - Smaller size with dual readouts.
  - Good for bench workflows involving multiple subcircuits.
- **Cons:**
  - Max current per channel commonly around 2A, limiting some higher power uses.
  - Accuracy and noise levels vary among products.

For electronics learners handling multiple small devices or modules, this can be a practical option.

---

## 3. Programmable Digital Benchtop Supply (Entry-Level)

Entry-level programmable supplies under $150 are rare but increasingly available from lesser-known manufacturers. These units allow basic programming of voltage/current sequences, useful for automating testing tasks.

- **Pros:**
  - Automate test sequences via USB or front panel.
  - Maintain stable outputs with software control.
  - Usually include memory presets.
- **Cons:**
  - Software interfaces often basic or buggy.
  - Build and component quality may be hit or miss.
  
This type is more suited for users with some test automation experience or those willing to experiment with scripting.

---

## 4. Linear Regulated Benchtop Supply (Low Noise Focused)

Linear supplies offer cleaner, lower-noise output compared to switching models, important for analog and RF circuits. While less common at this price point, a few basic linear benchtop supplies exist under $150.

- **Pros:**
  - Low output ripple and noise.
  - Simple and reliable technology.
  - Ideal for sensitive audio or instrumentation work.
- **Cons:**
  - Generally larger and heavier.
  - Lower efficiency – more heat generation.
  - Often limited current capabilities at this price.

If your project is sensitive to power noise and you don’t mind the bulk and heat, a basic linear supply can be a worthy choice.

---

## 5. Switching Mode Power Supplies (Compact and Efficient)

Many power supplies under $150 use switching technology, which are smaller, lighter, and more energy-efficient than linear ones. These are popular for hobbyists and classrooms due to size and cost advantages.

- **Pros:**
  - High efficiency and compact size.
  - Wide input voltage range.
  - Often packed with features like auto-recovery and USB charging outputs.
- **Cons:**
  - Higher output noise and ripple compared to linear types.
  - Potential electromagnetic interference (EMI).

Great all-rounder options for general electronics work and prototyping.

---

## 6. DIY or Kit-Based Benchtop Power Supplies

For learners and tinkerers, building your own bench power supply from a kit or open hardware design can deliver a highly customizable solution without a substantial price tag.

- **Pros:**
  - Deeply educational experience.
  - Customizable voltage/current limits and features.
  - Potential for upgrade or repair.
- **Cons:**
  - Requires assembly skills and tools.
  - Performance depends on components chosen.
  - Minimal user interface features unless DIY advanced panels.

Best suited for hobbyists interested in both electronics learning and power supply functionality.

## Final Thoughts

There are plenty of benchtop power supplies under $150 offering good value for home labs and light professional use. Your choice hinges on voltage/current needs, desired features, and noise tolerance.

For most casual users, a single or dual output switching supply with digital controls will be the best blend of affordability, convenience, and performance.

If your projects involve noise-sensitive electronics or you prioritize simplicity and linear regulation, keeping an eye out for budget linear units is worthwhile.

Whichever model you choose, focus on well-reviewed units and ensure basic safety protections are in place. A reliable benchtop power supply will enhance your electronics experience for years to come without busting your budget.
