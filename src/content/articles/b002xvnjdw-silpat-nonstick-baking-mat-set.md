# Silpat Baking Mat — 3-Year Cost Comparison vs. Parchment Paper

Parchment paper costs approximately $0.05–0.08 per sheet. A Silpat costs $20 per mat and is rated
for 2,000+ uses. At 10 uses per week, parchment costs $26–42 per year per baking session. The Silpat
pays for itself in 6–9 months.

## Performance differences

**Where Silpat wins:** Macarons, tuiles, and delicate cookies where even slight sticking ruins
the surface. The silicone matrix provides even more consistent non-stick performance than parchment.

**Where parchment wins:** High-fat baked goods (croissants, shortbread) where rendered butter
creates browning on the parchment that silicone doesn't absorb the same way. Also, any baking
that requires parchment overhang for lifting (brownies, bar cookies).

## Heat limits

Silpat is rated to 480°F. For pizza on a stone at 500°F+, use a peel — not a Silpat.
For standard baking (cookies, bread, vegetables), the mat handles the full range of home oven temps.

## Care

Hand wash only — dishwasher detergent degrades the silicone surface over time. Lay flat or roll
loosely for storage (don't fold; creasing damages the fiberglass mesh core).
