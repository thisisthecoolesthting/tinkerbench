## Fluke 117 vs Amprobe AM-570: When the 5x Price is Worth It

When it comes to buying a digital multimeter (DMM), you often face a range of choices—from budget models to premium tools with hefty price tags. Two popular models among professionals and serious hobbyists are the Fluke 117 and the Amprobe AM-570. The Fluke 117 typically costs close to five times more than the Amprobe AM-570. So, is that price difference justified? This article breaks down the key differences and helps you decide when the premium is worth paying.

---

## Quick Overview: Fluke 117 and Amprobe AM-570

- **Fluke 117**: A reliable, high-accuracy, True RMS DMM made by a market leader in electrical test tools. It’s designed for electricians working in commercial and residential environments, with features designed to save time and improve safety.

- **Amprobe AM-570**: A robust, versatile True RMS multimeter that offers a broad range of functions suited for general electrical and electronic troubleshooting at a significantly lower price point. A solid contender for users prioritizing value and decent performance.

---

## Price Differential and What It Means

With the Fluke 117 commanding around five times the price of the Amprobe AM-570, the question must be: what are you paying for? Beyond brand reputation, the price reflects differences in accuracy, build quality, ergonomic features, safety certifications, and special functions.

---

## Accuracy and True RMS Performance

Both meters provide True RMS measurements, which is crucial for accurate readings on non-linear loads and modern electronic devices.

- **Fluke 117**: Offers slightly better accuracy specifications, especially in voltage and current readings. This can be crucial in sensitive diagnostic scenarios or when adhering to tight tolerances in professional electrical work.

- **Amprobe AM-570**: Still True RMS, but with looser accuracy ratings. For general troubleshooting and most everyday applications, this difference is negligible.

If your work demands highly accurate data, particularly in commercial electrician jobs, the Fluke 117 pulls ahead.

---

## Build Quality and Durability

Fluke meters are engineered for tough job site conditions:

- **Fluke 117**: Known for rugged construction, impact resistance, and long-lasting reliability. The casing is designed to resist drops and rough handling. The buttons and dial feel precise and durable.

- **Amprobe AM-570**: While robust and well-built for the price, it feels less premium with lighter plastic and less refined controls. It’s durable enough for most workshop and home environments but might not withstand the heaviest wear and tear of industrial sites.

If you anticipate demanding field use and rough conditions, the Fluke’s premium build quality could justify the extra cost.

---

## Safety Ratings and Certifications

Safety matters when working with high voltages and industrial environments:

- **Fluke 117**: Typically rated CAT III 600 V / CAT IV 300 V, meeting stringent industrial safety standards. This means it can safely handle transient voltages from distribution panels and high-energy electrical environments.

- **Amprobe AM-570**: Usually rated CAT III 600 V but may lack some of the extra certifications or performance guarantees of Fluke. Still safe for most residential and commercial troubleshooting but less suitable if your work involves extremely high-energy circuits.

If safety certification levels are critical in your line of work, this is a key differentiator.

---

## Specialized Features and Ergonomics

The Fluke 117 offers several convenient features tailored for electricians:

- **Non-Contact Voltage Detection**: Allows quick verification of energized circuits without test leads.

- **Low Input Impedance Mode**: Reduces ghost voltage indications common in complex wiring to avoid false readings.

- **Backlit Display**: Bright, easy-to-read display for dark or cramped spaces.

- **Ergonomic Design**: Comfortable shape and well-placed buttons for prolonged use.

The Amprobe AM-570 is function-rich too but often lacks the subtle usability enhancements and quick diagnostics features of the Fluke.

---

## Range of Measurement Functions

Both meters cover essential parameters like:

- AC/DC voltage and current  
- Resistance  
- Continuity  
- Capacitance  
- Frequency  
- Diode testing  

The Amprobe AM-570 sometimes offers extra functions such as temperature measurement or a wider range in some tests while remaining budget-friendly. Evaluate which specific functions you need most—more functions at a lower price can make the Amprobe compelling if specialty features are a priority.

---

## Battery Life and Power Efficiency

The Fluke 117 tends to have longer battery life thanks to efficient power management and quality components. This can reduce downtime on the job and inconvenience.

The Amprobe will be fine for most tasks but might require replacing batteries more frequently in heavy usage scenarios.

---

## Calibration and Serviceability

Brand-name premium meters often come with an extended calibration guarantee or options for calibration services through authorized centers—important for regulated environments.

Fluke maintains strict calibration standards and offers excellent service and support.

Amprobe is serviceable but may have more limited options for calibration and technical support in some regions.

---

## When Is the 5x Price Worth It?

Consider the following scenarios for justifying the Fluke 117’s premium over the Amprobe AM-570:

- **Professional electricians in commercial or industrial settings** where safety certifications, rugged durability, and accuracy directly impact job quality and legal compliance.

- **Field technicians encountering harsh environments** who need non-contact voltage detection and reliable operation despite drops or rough handling.

- **Situations demanding fast diagnostics** where features like low impedance mode reduce troubleshooting time.

- **Businesses requiring traceable calibration** and warranty support to meet industry standards.

---

## When Is the Amprobe AM-570 a Better Fit?

The AM-570 is excellent when you:

- Are a hobbyist, student, or DIY enthusiast needing a reliable True RMS multimeter without breaking the bank.

- Need a versatile tool for electronics, automotive, or home electrical troubleshooting where the highest accuracy and certifications are less critical.

- Want more specialty measurement functions at a bargain without sacrificing core essential measurements.

- Don’t regularly work in hazardous environments demanding the highest safety ratings.

---

## Final Thoughts

Choosing between the Fluke 117 and Amprobe AM-570 boils down to your needs, environment, and budget. The Fluke model justifies its steep price through superior build, safety, accuracy, and thoughtful features that help professionals get the job done better and faster.

For many users, the Amprobe AM-570 offers excellent value with a broad feature set and respectable accuracy. It's a dependable, cost-effective option for everyday electrical testing.

If your work demands the utmost in safety, durability, and precision—especially in professional settings—the Fluke 117's premium features can pay dividends over time. But for most others, the budget-friendly Amprobe AM-570 delivers solid performance and versatility without blowing the budget.
