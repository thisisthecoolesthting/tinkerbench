ELEGOO UNO R3 Starter Kit — How Far Does It Actually Get You?

The UNO R3 is a clone of the Arduino Uno. Same ATmega328P chip, same pin layout, same IDE compatibility — at about 40% the cost of the official Genuino board. For learning and prototyping, the difference is zero.

What the kit includes (counted)

63 components across 19 types: LEDs, resistors, capacitors, a 1602 LCD, servo, ultrasonic sensor, IR receiver, relay, potentiometers, breadboard, and jumper wires. The 36-project tutorial PDF covers beginner through intermediate level.

Realistic project progression

Projects 1–10: basic I/O, digital and analog signals, serial communication — 1 weekend. Projects 11–25: sensors, LCD control, PWM, servo — 2–3 weeks. Projects 26–36: motor driver, IR control, sound — requires optional components not in kit.

What you’ll need next

After exhausting this kit, the natural next purchases are: a 9V power supply for standalone projects, an L298N motor driver module ($3), and additional sensors from the ELEGOO sensor kit (30 sensors for $15).
